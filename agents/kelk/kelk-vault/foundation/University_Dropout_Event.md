# University Dropout Event

This event details the period surrounding Chris's decision to leave university, which occurred around the age of 19 (2005).

**Context:** Chris was enrolled at Guelph-Humber for Journalism but found the environment unappealing and ultimately withdrew. Julian's family had taken Chris in for the summer before university after he was kicked out by both parents, an act of kindness Chris considers pivotal.
**Internal Conflict:** There was a significant internal conflict between the desire to embrace a 'hippie' counter-culture (reading Kerouac, thrift fashion, smoking) and the external pressure to pursue a conventional path (journalism for the Toronto Star).
**External Impact:** The decision caused significant distress for his parents, as it meant the RESP savings would be wasted. Both parents came together for one day to try to convince him to stay — he told them they would not succeed.
**The Break:** The situation was strained by the mental health crisis of Gwen, who attempted suicide by running away to die in a forest. This acted as a catalyst for Chris to withdraw, driven by anxiety and a feeling that the only path forward was away from school.
**Hindsight:** Chris views this decision with regret, recognizing it had long-term negative consequences. He identifies it as an early episode of feeling rejected and choosing what seemed like a 'justified' rejection in response. The real mistake, he believes, was made a year earlier by failing to prepare properly for university.
