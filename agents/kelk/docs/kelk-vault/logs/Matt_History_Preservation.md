# Detailed History: Friendship with Matt

I can take you through a quick history with my friend Matt. Feel free to ask follow-up questions. Matt was a central figure in my life between the ages of ~15 and ~25. Perhaps one of the longest friendships I have ever had. Then just radio silence. It is an enormous wound that I carry with me. I have stopped actively missing him but I will never not miss him. I met Matt during a Youth Orchestra (LJYO) excursion to Bowmanville in what I remember as the summer of 2000. Matt's family was one of the families hosting dinners for the traveling orchestra members, so I found myself at the dinner table with Matt, and also Kelly who I would eventually date. We didn't see each other after that until the orchestra season started that fall, and after that we were fast friends. By December 2000 we had formed a band with our friend Julian, and we spent much of that year at Julian's house trying to figure out how to play our guitars. Eventually we found a drummer in my friend Shanna's brother Randy, and Randy joined our band named The Agnostics. Matt and Jules and Randy and I played a lot. We rehearsed weekly, and tried to play at least monthly. In hindsight it was not enough to make real players out of us – we were league – but for us, in high school, it felt like we were doing it. I was very exciting about songwriting. Matt played Bass (which we figured was a natural fit because he started Cello at age 4), Jules played "lead" guitar (because he also played violin), Randy played drums, and I played "rhythm" guitar and sang because I love John Lennon even when he's making excuses about his guitar playing. Matt and I would spend HOURS on the phone watching videos online in the pre-youtube era – live performances by The Strokes were our favourite. We were obsessed. This was the core focus for a while, outside of school and piano and choir and orchestra. Real personal goals began to form in this young band. We won some talent shows, won some battles of the bands, but like many high school bands we crumbled as university approached. Julian moved away to school, and around the same time we decided Randy wasn't good enough as our drummer. This put us in a tailspin very quickly and the band dissolved. Matt and I remained friends through the orchestra – Matt was a younger than Jules and I, and I had made a mess of my high school transcript so I stayed back a year to pass English. Matt and I started writing together, and sharing demos extensively, and playing coffee houses and other random shows. Eventually I fell away into my 'activism' years, if that's what we're calling them, and I lost touch with Matt. ●●● That summer I had been kicked out by one parent and then the next parent, and found myself effectively homeless. Julian, home from school for the summer, agreed to take me in for the month so that I could prep before heading off to University. I had been accepted earlier in the spring of the year to Humber College, so together we moved from our Guelph-Humber for Journalism. Julian and his parents really saved me here – I don't know where I would be right now if they hadn't been so kind. Matt had also been accepted to Humber College, so together we moved from our (North and South (Matt) campuses. It only took a few weeks of orientation for us to start making the trip to visit each other on the other's campus.

Back to a quick history of my friendship with Matt: ... (cont'd) ... Weeks after Matt & I became accustomed to visiting each other's campus, my friend Gwen attempted suicide by running away to die in a forest. This freaked the shit out of me (luckily she was recovered and is safe). Adding to number of other factors, this inspired me to drop out of University. My parents were devastated. They came together for one day and one day only to try to convince me not to drop out, and I told them they were not going to convince me to stay. I felt it was the right decision at the time but it also felt like the only path forward. School didn't feel right. In hindsight, this is an early episode in feeling rejected and thereby choosing what seems to be a 'justified' rejection. In hindsight, the decision to drop out cost me a lot. But I also know that the real mistake was made a year earlier by failing to prepare for University. I neglected to plan and then rushed my applications, so in the end I probably got what I deserved. As you can tell, I have regrets. ... back to Matt: I moved in with my band in Peterborough, which was where I effectively "ran away to" after dropping out. Rent was about $210/month because there were 6 of us in a cheap rental house, so we took up lives as hippie-grifters and made just enough money to get by. We were regulars at the food banks (plural), we stole food from chain grocers, we got high and we rejected all alcohol. We thought we were living an alternative lifestyle. We were kind of just being bums. But we had fun doing it. ... Matt eventually started to visit us in Peterborough – which was a major culture shock for him. His family is very plain and docile – not many adventures, not much drama. Walking into a Peterborough slum with ornamental acrylic paint strewn all over the walls and a bunch of acidheads jamming in the basement was a bit of a shift for him. But then Matt started doing acid with us and became one of the family. This caused a rift in the band (The Cheshire Smile), because Matt was kind of seen as an outsider – and he was, except that he and I had more or less become inseparables. Eventually The Cheshire Smile split – Michelle and Brandon left to go have a child, and Dave and Andy and I started a new band with Matt, called Mariposa. Matt wasn't really able to be part of the band for the first early weeks because of his school schedule. But by the time summer 2006 came around, Dave & Andy & I had rented our own Peterborough slum and had taken jobs in anticipation of moving to Toronto to join Matt there. Meanwhile, Matt was out of school for the summer and free to come make music and have adventures with us in Peterborough. This was a wild time. In September 2006 Matt's parents co-signed on a bungalow in Toronto's High Park neighbourhood ($1800 rent, so $450 a piece – unbelievable. There were only three bedrooms so I took the storage closet in the basement to make it "work"). The four of us moved in and suddenly we had access to being a BAND seven days a week. We rehearsed in our basement. Our neighbours hated it. We didn't care. We spent every night out in the city in some way – seeing shows, or playing shows – and then we would come home and keep writing together. Matt and I had established a formal co-writer relationship by this point, but ironically much of the work was being written by Matt (alone) and then either Me and Dave, or Me and Andy, or Me and Andy (together). This dynamic would continue to shift, but I only ever sought "formal" partnership with Matt, owing largely to a massive Lennon-McCartney debt. In October 2007, we moved out of our bungalow and into a 1500 square-foot factory warehouse. Dave had been kicked out of the band in spring 2007 (sadly), so it was only Andy and Matt and I that moved into the warehouse. We were performing at that time as a three-piece version of Mariposa. That effort disbanded in March 2008, and we had no idea what we were going to do. I think Matt was panicking a little bit – maybe we're all panicking – not knowing how to push forward from there. At least Matt was still in school – I had only just started the job that would later feed into my eventual broadcast career. No one really knew what was happening. Then in August 2008, Andy and I decided to get blindingly drunk one day and just not stop recording until an EP was finished. We knocked it out in a day – and we knew that the "Transubstantiated Orphan Under a Harvest Moon" EP was very special. So we made a bunch of cassette copies and started handing them around and getting very enthusiastic responses. The most enthusiastic of all came from Matt – who could not stop raving about how much he loved the tape. Matt was so excited. Andy and I were proud of our work, and both thought it was pretty funny that all it took was a case of beer, a single microphone, and an old Compaq laptop. Matt's excitement made it legitimate though. The excitement from our other friends and acquaintances made it legitimate. We knew we had found a new band, somehow, and there was no choice but to form it. Heartbeat Hotel. (I will leave it here, as not to overwhelm you.)
