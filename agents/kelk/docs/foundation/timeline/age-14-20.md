# Age 14-20: The Breaking

**Status: COMPLETE** — Richest coverage in transcript

## The Divorce (Age 14, Year 2000)

### The Day

Chris and James received notes at school: "Don't come home, go to Shauna and Randy's house." They met up, compared notes, went to the friend's house. Later, Cathy picked them up for James's hockey game.

At the hockey game, Chris asked what was happening. Cathy explained:
- Earlier that day, Michael went to her office and "yelled embarrassing things about her"
- They drove home together
- At home, there was an assault — Michael hit/threw things, Cathy felt endangered
- Police were called
- Michael was arrested, restraining order issued

### The Restaurant

That same evening, Chris saw Michael at a restaurant (circumstances unclear — did he go looking for him?). Michael confirmed everything. Then faces appeared around the corner:
- John Tapscott (Cathy's friend)
- Maternal grandmother

Chris immediately understood: "They were there to see if I was OK. My heart just sunk. My dad was seen as a threat."

**This moment** — the realization that his father was now viewed as dangerous — is described as foundational trauma.

### The Affair

The divorce was triggered by Cathy's affair with Dan (Marie's husband). The La Jeunesse community was torn apart:
- Hostility between Michael and Dan
- Hostility between Cathy and Marie
- The 8-family ecosystem collapsed
- Chris and James caught in the middle

### Custody Chaos

Post-divorce living arrangements:
1. Initially stayed with Cathy at family home
2. Chris ran away out of frustration
3. James followed
4. Both went to live with Michael
5. James went back to Cathy initially
6. Both ended up at Michael's for months
7. Michael was "nasty" to James → James moved back to Cathy
8. Chris stayed with Michael, they moved together
9. Chris ran away from Michael's (he was nasty to Chris too)
10. Back to Cathy's

The "family home" became associated with dishonesty. Chris preferred being at Michael's despite everything.

### The Piano Battle

Symbolic fight over the piano:
- Piano was at family home (now Cathy's)
- Michael's rented apartment had "a piece of garbage piano"
- Piano represented the music community that collapsed
- Chris eventually switched from piano to guitar — "I needed a portable instrument"

## The Hardening

Chris describes developing defense mechanisms:
- Withdrawal
- Isolation
- "Safety maneuvers"
- Presenting as "happy and energetic" while internally hardened

These mechanisms were initially protective but became alienating to others.

## Michael's Crisis

After the divorce:
- Michael suffered severe depression
- Wasn't leaving the house
- Chris felt responsible to take care of him
- Chris saw Michael as the victim (though unsure in hindsight)
- Michael's mother (Rollie) visited often

### The Ellie Incident

Michael started dating Ellie's mother — Ellie being James's girlfriend. James was furious. This drove a wedge between James and Michael, and between James and Chris (who stayed with Michael). James moved back to Cathy's.

## Relationships

### Kelly

- Met in Youth Orchestra (Michael directed it)
- Chris played clarinet, Kelly played violin
- Started dating fall 2002 (Chris age 16, Kelly age 17)
- ~1.5 years together, possibly close to 2 years
- Long-distance (40 km apart), lots of phone time
- First date: jazz club
- Peak memory: Paul McCartney concert, May 2002, Air Canada Centre
  - Stayed at uncle's place in Toronto
  - "Held each other all night"
  - Both cried during "Hey Jude" (song about child of divorce)
- Ended: Chris cheated by kissing another girl, they stayed together briefly, then Kelly went to university and left him for another guy

Kelly was serious about music:
- Violinist and pianist
- Into Jimi Hendrix, put effects on violin
- Went to university for violin

### Kate

Childhood friend (Marie and Dan's daughter). After the divorce, Chris explicitly told Kate they couldn't be friends anymore due to "divorce weirdness." This is a source of ongoing shame.

Gift-buying for Kate mentioned in recent transcript — suggests some level of contact resumed, but details unclear.

### Matt

Bandmate in The Agnostics. Described as very close — "inseparable," shared all jokes, all moments. The end of this friendship was a major blow to Chris's confidence. He expected it to be a "forever relationship." It wasn't.

## Activism: Gaian Intelligence Collective

High school activist group, grades 11-12 (~2003-2004):

**Three campaigns:**
1. Opposition to uranium enrichment at local nuclear reactor
2. Ban pesticides on lawns (successful — local pesticide ban passed)
3. Awareness of US-Canada military authority merger

**Format:**
- Monthly "scenes" (performances/protests)
- ~100 student participation at peak
- Lasted one summer only

**Significance:**
- Described as "liberating"
- Provided sense of agency
- Counter-culture identity formation

## University Dropout (Age 19, 2005)

- Enrolled at University of Guelph-Humber (journalism program), Sept 2005
- Matt enrolled at Humber South Campus same time
- Dropped out after ~2 months
- Moved to Peterborough with band by Nov 2005 (Murray St.)
- Motivations:
  - Counter-culture/anti-establishment ideals
  - Resentment toward parents' sacrifice ("they're paying for this")
  - Commitment to band
  - "Distraught from divorce," "very into counterculture," "not open to university"

Chris reflects: "Right decision at the time, but university was more productive in hindsight."
